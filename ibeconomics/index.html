IB Economics · Interactive diagrams

The whole set

Every diagram in the IB Economics syllabus, built to be interactive. Drag curves, slide things, watch the dynamics. Change the setting from a St Andrews greengrocer to Albanian wheat to see the same logic survive.

Diagrams are grouped by the canvas they share — the underlying plot type and axes. A student who has learned one diagram on a canvas will find the others immediately readable.

AD/AS · price level × real output

  • AD curve and shifts 3.2 · RequiredBaseline AD with seven shift-cause chips (C, I, G, X−M factors); ghost baseline stays visible when shifted; component-change readout in right panel.
  • SRAS curve and shifts 3.2 · RequiredUpward-sloping SRAS with seven shift-cause chips (oil shock, wages, productivity, indirect tax, harvest conditions); right-panel cost-factor readout.
  • Alternative views of AS (Keynesian vs neoclassical) 3.2 · RequiredThree archetypes on one canvas — pure Keynesian (L-shaped), intermediate (three ranges), pure neoclassical (vertical at Yf). Shift AD to see how the three schools predict different outcomes.
  • Macroeconomic equilibrium (SR and LR) 3.2 · RequiredAD, SRAS, and LRAS on one canvas. Four shock scenarios (no shock, AD boom, AD bust, SRAS shock / stagflation). Time slider animates wage adjustment so students see the long-run return to Yf.
  • Economic growth via AD or LRAS 3.3 · RequiredThree growth-source modes: AD-led (growth + inflation), LRAS-led (real, sustainable growth, often deflationary), and matched. Magnitude slider to explore how much.
  • Demand-pull inflation 3.3 · RequiredFive-stage walkthrough of AD-driven inflation: start at LR eq → AD shifts right → inflationary gap → SRAS adjusts → new LR eq at higher PL. Four real-world causes.
  • Cost-push inflation 3.3 · RequiredFive-stage walkthrough of SRAS-driven inflation: LR eq → SRAS shifts left → stagflation → SRAS recovers → new LR eq. Four causes (oil, wages, supply chain, depreciation).
  • Deflation & deflationary gap 3.3 · RequiredAD-left story with two paths forward: classical (wages fall → SRAS shifts right → recovery at lower PL) vs Keynesian (wages sticky → persistent gap and cyclical unemployment). Four real-world cases (Japan 1990s, eurozone crisis, balance-sheet recession, austerity).
  • Stabilisation policy: monetary, fiscal, supply-side 3.5, 3.6, 3.7 · RequiredPick a starting condition (recessionary gap / inflationary gap / at Yf) and a policy (monetary, fiscal, supply-side) with direction + strength. Watch which curve shifts, the gap close (or overshoot, or worsen), with transmission chain and side effects spelled out in the right panel.
  • Crowding-out effect 3.6 · HL RequiredTwo-panel diagram: money market (Md shifts right, i rises) and AD-AS (intended vs net AD shift). Sliders for fiscal-expansion size and investment sensitivity. Severity scales from negligible to near-complete.
