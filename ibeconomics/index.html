IB Economics · Interactive diagrams

The whole set

A collection of interactive diagrams covering every required graphical element of the IB Economics syllabus, designed for use after teacher introduction, in class play, and at revision time. Drag things. Release them. Change the setting from a St Andrews greengrocer to Albanian wheat and see how the same logic survives.

Unit 2 · Microeconomics

  • Market equilibrium 2.3 · Required Demand, supply, equilibrium, shortages and surpluses, with springy return to equilibrium.
  • Price elasticity of demand 2.5 · Required Constant PED (perfectly elastic/inelastic/unitary) and the linear demand curve.
  • Indirect tax 2.7 · Required Specific and ad valorem taxes, tax incidence, welfare loss.
  • Subsidy 2.7 · Required Subsidy incidence, cost to government, welfare effects.
  • Price ceilings and price floors 2.7 · Required Maximum and minimum prices, with stickiness and black-market pressure.
  • Negative externalities of production & consumption 2.8 · Required MSC vs MPC, overproduction, welfare loss, Pigouvian tax response.
  • Positive externalities of production & consumption 2.8 · Required MSB vs MPB, underconsumption, subsidy and provision responses.
