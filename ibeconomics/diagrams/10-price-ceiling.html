Price ceiling

Real-world case
A price ceiling is a government-mandated maximum price. Drag the dashed line down below equilibrium to see the ceiling bind.
Slider shows ceiling as % of equilibrium price. Push it to 100% or above and the ceiling becomes non-binding. Below that, the ceiling bites. You can also drag the dashed line on the plot directly.

A price ceiling at or above the equilibrium price has no effect. Pulled below equilibrium, it creates a shortage: buyers want more at the lower price, but sellers are willing to supply less. The market can't clear through price.

Key idea. Price ceilings transfer some surplus from producers to some consumers (those who can still buy). But the economy loses output overall, because the shortage prevents beneficial trades. The missing trades are the deadweight loss.
