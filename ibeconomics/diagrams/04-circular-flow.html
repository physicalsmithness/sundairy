The circular flow of income

Households spend on domestic goods.
Firms pay wages, rent, interest, profits.
Out of households' income, taxes go to government.
Government buys from domestic firms.

Income flows in a circle: firms pay households for factors of production, households spend on firms' goods. Every injection (G, I, X) adds to the flow; every leakage (T, S, M) pulls out of it. In the long run, the two must balance — that's macroeconomic equilibrium.

Key idea. The economy is a circuit, not a collection of isolated transactions. A change in one flow ripples through the others. Leakages equal injections in equilibrium; when they don't, the economy is expanding or contracting.
