Click a button above to see what happens. The difference between a price change and a non-price change is one of the most commonly confused ideas in economics.

The key distinction. A change in price of the good itself causes a movement along the demand curve — the curve doesn't move. A change in any other factor (income, tastes, price of related goods, number of buyers, expectations) causes the entire demand curve to shift.
