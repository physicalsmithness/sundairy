Free trade with imports and exports

Scenario
"Autarky" is the no-trade benchmark: price settles where domestic S = D. This is where the market would sit if the country couldn't trade with anyone. Slide the world price to open up trade.
Pw below autarky price → country imports. Pw above → country exports. The small-country assumption: the country is a price-taker; its Pw is given by world markets.

At autarky price, domestic S meets domestic D. Both are satisfied within the country with no trade.

Who wins and who loses. When a country opens up to trade:
If it imports (Pw < autarky P), consumers win (pay less, get more), domestic producers lose (sell less at lower price), but consumer gain > producer loss — net welfare rises.
If it exports (Pw > autarky P), producers win (sell more at higher price), domestic consumers lose (buy less at higher price), but producer gain > consumer loss — net welfare rises.
Trade always raises total welfare in this model, but it redistributes.
