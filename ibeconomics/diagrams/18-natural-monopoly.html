Natural monopoly

Regulation
In a natural monopoly, ATC declines over the whole relevant range — huge fixed costs spread over more and more units. One big firm is always cheaper than many small ones. Examples: water supply, rail networks, electricity distribution.
How big is the unavoidable fixed cost? Water-pipe networks have enormous fixed costs relative to flow.

Unregulated, the natural monopolist picks q where MR = MC — a small quantity at a high price, extracting large profit. The huge ATC at low q means consumers face very high prices.

The regulation dilemma. P = MC is allocatively efficient — but ATC > MC here, so the firm would make a loss and need a subsidy (taxpayers cover the fixed costs). P = ATC ("fair-return") lets the firm break even without a subsidy, closer to but not quite at the allocatively efficient quantity. Both beat the unregulated outcome. Which to pick is a political and practical question.
