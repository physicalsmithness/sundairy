Cost-push inflation

Cause
Cost-push inflation: firms' production costs rise, so SRAS shifts left. Price level rises AND real output falls — the painful combination called stagflation.
1. Start at LR equilibrium
2. SRAS shifts left
3. Stagflation: PL up, Y down
4. Wages fall, SRAS recovers (slowly)
5. Back to Yf, PL higher

Cost-push is the painful one. Unlike demand-pull (which at least boosts output temporarily), cost-push inflation brings inflation and falling output simultaneously — the dreaded stagflation. Central banks face a dilemma: tighten to fight inflation and you deepen the recession; loosen to help output and you feed inflation further. The 1970s oil shocks were the canonical example.
