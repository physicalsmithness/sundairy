With no trade, each country consumes what it produces — somewhere on its own PPC. Pick "Specialise" to send each country to the vertex of the good it has a comparative advantage in. Then "Specialise + trade" lets both countries consume outside their PPCs.

Comparative advantage, not absolute advantage. Albania can make more wheat than Brazil can (100 vs 60). Albania can also make less cloth than Brazil (50 vs 60). You might think: Albania is better at wheat, Brazil is better at cloth, trade is obvious. But the deep insight from Ricardo (1817) is that trade still benefits both countries even if one has absolute advantage in both — as long as the opportunity costs differ. Each country should specialise where its opp cost is lowest, then trade.
