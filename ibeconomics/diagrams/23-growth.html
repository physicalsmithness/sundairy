Economic growth: AD-led vs LRAS-led

Growth source
AD-led growth comes from higher demand — stimulus, credit booms, confidence. But when the economy is near Yf, AD growth mostly produces inflation, not real output. LRAS-led growth comes from actual capacity increases — more labour, capital, or better technology. It's real and sustainable.

AD shifts right: some output growth, some inflation — the balance depends on how close we are to full employment. At or near Yf, most of an AD rise becomes inflation.

Only LRAS-led growth is sustainable. Moving AD right without expanding capacity is like running a hot engine: you get more output short-term, but it overheats (inflation) and eventually reverts. Shifting LRAS right moves the whole ceiling up: permanent gains in real output, no inflation cost. This is why real-income growth over decades comes from productivity and capacity, not stimulus.
