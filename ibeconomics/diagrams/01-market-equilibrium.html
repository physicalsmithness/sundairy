The market is in equilibrium. Every apple a customer wants to buy at this price is matched by an apple a seller is willing to sell. No one is frustrated.

Key idea. Equilibrium is the price at which quantity demanded equals quantity supplied. It is not imposed; it emerges because any other price leaves someone unhappy, and unhappy buyers or sellers keep pushing the price until the mismatch is gone.

How to play

Drag the black dashed horizontal price line up or down to set a price away from equilibrium. Shortages and surpluses appear on the diagram. Let go, and the market snaps back. Drag the round handles at the ends of the demand or supply curves to shift them around, or use the sliders below the graph for shifts and changes in elasticity. The scenario dropdown at the top re-labels everything to a different market without changing the underlying economics.
