With no shock, AD, SRAS, and LRAS all meet at the same point (Yf, baseline PL). Apply a shock and watch the short-run equilibrium diverge from Yf, creating an output gap. Then advance time to see wages adjust and the economy self-correct.

The long-run neutrality of AD. AD shifts change real output only in the short run. Eventually, wages and other input prices adjust, SRAS shifts to restore Yf, and the only lasting effect is on the price level. This is the core neoclassical result. Keynesians agree with the mechanism but argue "the long run" can take decades — in the meantime real people are unemployed.
