Scenario

A tariff protects domestic producers by raising the effective price of imports. Consumers pay more, consume less; producers sell more at a higher price; government collects tariff revenue. But two triangles of welfare are lost completely.

Who wins and who loses from a tariff. Producers win (higher price, more market share). Government wins (tariff revenue). Consumers lose (higher price, less to consume). And there are two DWL triangles — welfare that simply vanishes: the production DWL (domestic producers making what foreigners could make cheaper) and the consumption DWL (consumers who valued the good between the two prices and now can't afford it).
