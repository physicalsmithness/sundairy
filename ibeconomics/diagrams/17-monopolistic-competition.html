Monopolistic competition

Scenario
Each firm faces its own downward-sloping D because products are differentiated (coffee shops, branded clothing, restaurants). Short-run: profit or loss possible like a monopoly. Long-run: free entry shifts each firm's D leftward until D is tangent to ATC — zero economic profit, but at a Q below min-ATC, so resources are wasted.
Where does this firm's demand sit on the plot? Moves its whole demand curve up or down (parallel shift of D and MR).

At a demand intercept of £1.20, the firm picks q where MR = MC, then charges the highest price D allows. With costs as shown, there's currently positive economic profit — the green rectangle. In the long run, entry by competitors will shift this firm's D left until profit is zero.

Excess capacity theorem. In the long run, D must be tangent to ATC (zero profit). Because ATC is U-shaped and tangent to a downward-sloping D, the tangency sits on ATC's downward-sloping side — to the left of ATC's minimum. The firm produces less than the quantity that would minimise average cost. This "excess capacity" is a productive inefficiency, the price we pay for product variety.
