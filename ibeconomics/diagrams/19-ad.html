Aggregate demand

Cause a shift
The aggregate demand curve shows total planned spending on a country's output at each price level: AD = C + I + G + (X − M). It slopes downward because of the real-balance, interest-rate, and net-export effects. Pick a cause to see AD shift.

Aggregate demand has four components: consumer spending C, business investment I, government spending G, and net exports X − M. Anything that changes one of these shifts the whole AD curve.

Shifts vs movements. Changes in the price level produce movements along AD (because of real-balance, interest-rate, and net-export effects). Changes in any of the four components shift the entire curve. Distinguishing the two is one of the most common stumbling blocks in macro diagrams.
