Income elasticity of demand measures how quantity demanded responds to changes in income: %ΔQd / %ΔY. A positive YED means more is bought as income rises (normal good); a negative YED means less is bought (inferior good).

Three categories. Luxury goods have YED > 1: quantity rises faster than income. Necessity goods have 0 < YED < 1: quantity rises but slower than income. Inferior goods have YED < 0: quantity actually falls as income rises, because buyers switch to more expensive substitutes they can now afford.
