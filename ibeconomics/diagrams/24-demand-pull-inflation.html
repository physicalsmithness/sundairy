Demand-pull inflation

Cause
Demand-pull inflation: AD grows faster than the economy's productive capacity, bidding up prices. The classic "too much money chasing too few goods" story.
1. Start at LR equilibrium
2. AD shifts right
3. PL rises, Y above Yf
4. SRAS shifts left (wages adjust)
5. Back to Yf, but PL much higher

What's happening

Real output Y 120
Price level PL 100
Inflation vs start 0%
Start at long-run equilibrium. Click the stages in order to see demand-pull inflation play out.

Demand-pull vs cost-push. Demand-pull inflation comes from the demand side (AD rising) and initially boosts real output along with prices. Cost-push inflation comes from the supply side (SRAS falling) and brings inflation with falling output — stagflation. Demand-pull is "a nice problem to have"; cost-push is painful.
