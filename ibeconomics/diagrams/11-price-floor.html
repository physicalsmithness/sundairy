Price floor

Real-world case
A price floor is a government-mandated minimum price. Drag the dashed line up above equilibrium to see the floor bind.
Slider shows floor as % of equilibrium price. At 100% or below the floor is non-binding. Above that, excess supply appears. You can also drag the dashed line on the plot directly.
The unsold quantity (excess supply) has to go somewhere. Pick a consequence to see a brief explanation.

A price floor at or below the equilibrium price has no effect. Pushed above equilibrium, it creates excess supply: sellers offer more at the higher price than buyers are willing to buy. The market can't clear through price.

Key idea. Price floors transfer some surplus from consumers to producers who still manage to sell. But the economy loses output overall, because the excess supply prevents beneficial trades and unsold stock is wasted or purchased by government. The missing trades are the deadweight loss.
