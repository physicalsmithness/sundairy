Scenario

An export subsidy boosts producers' effective price per exported unit. But to keep producers willing to sell in the domestic market, the domestic price has to rise too. So domestic consumers pay more, domestic output rises, exports grow, and the government writes a cheque. Both triangles of welfare are lost.

Why export subsidies are especially controversial. Unlike tariffs (where government at least collects revenue), export subsidies cost the domestic government money AND harm domestic consumers AND distort world markets — foreign producers complain about "dumping". The WTO has strict rules against most export subsidies, and the US and EU have fought long-running disputes over agricultural export subsidies. Net national welfare effect is unambiguously negative.
