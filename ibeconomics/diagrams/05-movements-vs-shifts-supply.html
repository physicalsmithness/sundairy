Click a button above to see what happens. The difference between a price change and a non-price change is one of the most commonly confused ideas in economics.

The key distinction. A change in price of the good itself causes a movement along the supply curve — the curve doesn't move. A change in any other factor (input costs, technology, number of firms, expected future prices, producer subsidies or taxes) causes the entire supply curve to shift.
