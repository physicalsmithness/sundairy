The business cycle with trend

Scenario
Real GDP rises over time (trend growth) but not smoothly — it oscillates around the trend in cycles of expansion and contraction. The business cycle is this oscillation; the trend is the underlying long-run growth.
Slide along the timeline. The right panel tells you which phase of the cycle you're in and how GDP compares to trend.

Expansion phase: actual GDP is growing and is above (or approaching) the trend. Unemployment falling, inflation pressure building.

Four phases. Expansion: GDP rising, unemployment falling, inflation pressure building. Peak: growth slows as capacity constraints bite. Contraction (recession if it lasts two quarters): GDP falling, unemployment rising. Trough: bottom of the cycle. Then another expansion begins. The trend reflects long-run productivity and capacity growth — what LRAS shifts would produce over time.
