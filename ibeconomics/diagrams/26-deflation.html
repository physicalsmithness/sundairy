Deflation and the deflationary gap

Cause
Deflation: the price level actually falls. AD weakens, and if the economy's wages are sticky downward (the Keynesian case), output falls below Yf and stays there — a persistent deflationary gap with cyclical unemployment.

2. AD shifts left
3. Recessionary gap: PL down, Y down
4. Classical: wages fall, SRAS right
5. Keynesian: wages sticky, gap persists

Why deflation is dangerous. Falling prices sound nice to consumers — but they're lethal for an economy. Real debt burdens rise (you owe the same nominal amount but earn less nominal income), households delay purchases (why buy now when it'll be cheaper next month?), and firms cut wages and investment. The result is a self-reinforcing spiral. The deflationary gap is the distance between actual Y and Yf; cyclical unemployment is the associated jobless workers. If wages are sticky downward (Keynesian view), this gap can persist for years.
