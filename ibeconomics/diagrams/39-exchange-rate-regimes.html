Under a floating exchange rate regime, the market determines the exchange rate. The central bank doesn't intervene. Shifts in demand or supply simply move the rate to a new clearing level.

The trilemma of exchange rates. A country can pick two of: (1) a fixed exchange rate, (2) independent monetary policy, (3) free capital movement. It can't have all three. Fixed regimes (like China's peg) sacrifice monetary independence. Floating regimes (like the UK and US) accept volatility in the exchange rate. Managed regimes try to split the difference — usually with limited success in extreme stress.
