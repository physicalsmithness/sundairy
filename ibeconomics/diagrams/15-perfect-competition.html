Perfect competition: firm and industry

Scenario
In perfect competition the firm is a price-taker: market price is set in the industry diagram on the right, and the firm's demand curve is horizontal at that price. Profit-max output is where MC = MR = price.
Slide to change the industry price. Watch how the firm's optimal quantity, profit or loss, and shut-down decision change.
In the long run, entry (if firms profit) or exit (if firms lose) drives price to the minimum of ATC, giving zero economic profit.

At price £0.80, the firm produces where MC = P. Average cost at that quantity is lower than price, so the firm makes a positive economic profit (the green rectangle).

Key idea. A firm in perfect competition is a price-taker: it faces a horizontal demand curve at the market price (because its output is tiny relative to the industry and substitutes are perfect). Profit-max is always where MC = price. In the long run, free entry or exit drives price to the minimum of ATC — zero economic profit, productive efficiency, allocative efficiency.
