Stabilisation policy tries to close an output gap. Expansionary policies shift AD right (or LRAS right for supply-side). Contractionary policies shift AD left. The three policy families — monetary, fiscal, supply-side — differ in who wields them, how quickly they work, and what side effects they have.

The policy dilemma. Monetary policy works through interest rates — fast to change but weak when rates hit zero ("the liquidity trap") or when banks won't lend. Fiscal policy works through direct government spending or taxes — more reliable in a deep recession, but slow (political delays), and adds to public debt. Supply-side policy takes years to work but is the only way to raise Yf itself rather than just moving AD around a fixed Yf.
