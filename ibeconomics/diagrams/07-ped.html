Price elasticity of demand measures how sharply quantity demanded responds to price: %ΔQ / %ΔP. A steeper demand curve is more inelastic — quantity doesn't move much when price changes. A flatter curve is more elastic — quantity moves a lot.

The revenue insight. If demand is inelastic (PED < 1), total revenue moves with price: raise price, revenue rises. If demand is elastic (PED > 1), revenue moves against price: raise price, revenue falls. At unitary PED, revenue is unchanged.
