Externalities

Type of externality
When production or consumption of a good affects third parties, private and social cost (or benefit) diverge. The free market over- or under-produces relative to the socially optimal level.
How big is the external cost or benefit per unit? Slide to zero to remove the externality.

In a negative production externality, firms ignore the costs they impose on third parties (pollution, noise, congestion). Social cost exceeds private cost, so the market over-produces relative to the socially optimal level. The welfare loss triangle captures this over-production.

Key idea. Private decisions use private costs and benefits. Social optimum uses social costs and benefits. The gap is where markets fail. Pigouvian taxes (for negative externalities) and subsidies (for positive ones) try to close this gap by making firms or consumers face the full social cost or benefit.
